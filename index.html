CS:GO Data Visualisation

Counter Strike: Global Offensive, widely known as CS:GO, is a FPS (First Person Shooting) Game. CS:GO is the most up-to-date release of the Counter Strike line-up. Since its first release in 2012, it has attracted on average of 11 Million Monthly Users.

CS:GO is a highly competitive game where players and teams worlwide rely on hours and hours of practice, to excel on map mastery, team chemistry, timing, in-game strategy, aiming accuracy and reaction time.

The following represents data acquired from the Official SportsData.io Website, detailing the popularity of CS:GO across the world, the demand of the game across its lifespan, the most dominant teams, and the most favoured and hated maps in the game.

How Well-Known Has CS:GO Been Across The World ?

CS:GO is a highly demanded game with many players across the world. From 2015 to 2020, In United States of America alone, there are 7850 competitive matches distributed across 618 major pro events.

Followed by Canada, Australia, Russia and China at 6027, 3813, 5675 and 2415 total competitive matches consecutively.

Despite the fact that Russia had held more competitions over time, the demand in Australia and China is relatively larger than that in Russia.

This can be shown by them holding more matches per event, indicating that the events being held are larger in size, which means the stadium and streaming quality need to account for more teams and viewers.

How Popular Has CS:GO Been Over Its Lifespan ?

CS:GO has been very well-kwown for its aiming difficulty and the small hitboxes, making the game very difficult for beginners to play.

With the help of Internet Marketing and Streamers, In 2015 to 2016, CS:GO popularity increases dramatically, over 300% increase in the total number of events held in the top 10 countries.

The popularity of CS:GO, peaked during 2017-2018 at an astonishing number of 200 competitive events in average being held across the top 10 countries.

In 2019 and 2020, the popularity of CS:GO starts to decline, this is caused by many similar FPS games started to establish their branding and presence with more modern UI and easier game mechanics.

Moreover, during this period of time, CS:GO have been crowded by cheaters and Valve not investing enough in engineering reliable anti-cheat algorithms.

The Most Favourite Maps In CS:GO

There are many controversies revolving around CS:GO maps, many non-professional players exploit the fact that some of the maps are imbalanced, favouring either terrorists or counter-terrorists during matches.

In professional scenes, the most favourite map to play is Mirage, Inferno, and Train being played at 3188, 2951, and 2630 matches consecutively.

These maps are favoured because, they are relatively larger than other maps and provide the players more possible of strategies to execute.

Therefore, providing a more balanced gameplay between either side of the oppositions.

The Most Hated Maps In CS:GO

In CS:GO professional scenes, before any matches, both sides of the oppositions are given a choice of 7 maps to play and each will be given 3 chances to remove a map alternately. Leaving 1 remaining map to play.

As we can see, the most removed map is Nuke, being removed at first choice at a total of 6416 times. Followed by Overpass at 4939 times.

The chart shows that Vertigo and Cobblestone as the least hated map of the game. But it is not true, these 2 maps are just so imbalanced that most professional CS:GO matches do not include them as a part of the choice.

Who Are The Most Dominant Teams In CS:GO ?

During the early rise of CS:GO popularity, the most dominant team is Cloud9 winning a total of 166 professional games in 2016 alone.

In 2017, some other major CS:GO teams such as Tricked, Liquid, and Faze entered the top 4 leaderboard competing with Cloud9 as the number 1 spot with a slight 8 win difference. With Cloud9 still leading the pack with 152 total wins.

In 2018 onwards, Cloud9 popularity dramatically declines, this is caused by bad management and player performance. In 2018, Astralis took the number 1 spot from Cloud9 with a tremendous 207 total wins.

Tricked became the number one team in 2019 with 205 total wins followed by liquid at 171 total wins.

In 2020, the total wins of all the teams dropped because there are no major professional events being held after the decline of CS:GO popularity, competition, and a hit from global pandemic.

Overall, the teams with the least performance fluctuation overtime are Liquid, fnatic, and mousesports.
